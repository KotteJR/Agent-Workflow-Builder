# REGULATION (EU) No. 1308/2013

**Adopted:** December 17, 2013

**Subject:** Establishing a common organisation of the markets in agricultural products and repealing Council Regulations (EEC) No. 922/72, (EEC) No. 234/79, (EC) No. 1037/2001 and (EC) No. 1234/2007

## PART I - INTRODUCTORY PROVISIONS

## ARTICLE 1 - Scope of Application

### 1. General Scope

This Regulation establishes the common organisation of the markets in agricultural products for all the products listed in Annex I to the Treaties, with the exception of fishery and aquaculture products as defined in the Union legislation on the common organisation of the markets in fishery and aquaculture products.

### 2. Agricultural Product Sectors

The agricultural products referred to in paragraph 1 are subdivided into the following sectors, as listed in the corresponding sections of Annex I:

- **a)** Cereals - Part I
- **b)** Rice - Part II
- **c)** Sugar - Part III
- **d)** Dried fodder - Part IV
- **e)** Seeds for sowing - Part V
- **f)** Hops - Part VI
- **g)** Olive oil and table olives - Part VII
- **h)** Flax and hemp - Part VIII
- **i)** Fruit and vegetables - Part IX
- **j)** Processed fruit and vegetable products - Part X
- **k)** Bananas - Part XI
- **l)** Wine - Part XII
- **m)** Live trees and other plants, bulbs, roots and similar products, cut flowers and ornamental foliage - Part XIII
- **n)** Tobacco - Part XIV
- **o)** Beef - Part XV
- **p)** Milk and milk products - Part XVI
- **q)** Pork - Part XVII
- **r)** Sheep and goat meat - Part XVIII
- **s)** Eggs - Part XIX
- **t)** Poultry meat - Part XX
- **u)** Agricultural ethyl alcohol - Part XXI
- **v)** Apiculture products - Part XXII
- **w)** Silkworms - Part XXIII
- **x)** Other products - Part XXIV

## ARTICLE 2 - General Provisions of the Common Agricultural Policy (CAP)

For measures provided for in this Regulation, Regulation (EU) 2021/2116 of the European Parliament and of the Council and the provisions laid down thereunder shall apply.

## ARTICLE 3 - Definitions

### 1. Specific Definitions

For the purposes of this Regulation, the definitions contained in Annex II, which concern specific sectors, shall apply.

### 2. Common Definitions

For the purposes of this Regulation, the definitions provided in Regulation (EU) 2021/2116 and Regulation (EU) 2021/2115 of the European Parliament and the Council shall apply, unless otherwise provided in this Regulation.

### 3. Less Developed Regions

For the purposes of this Regulation, "less developed regions" means the regions as defined in Article 90(2), first subparagraph, point (a), of Regulation (EU) No. 1303/2013.

### 4. Adverse Climate Events

"Adverse climatic events that may be regarded as natural disasters" means conditions such as frost, hail, ice, rain or drought that destroy more than 30% of either:
- The average annual production of a given farmer for the previous three-year period, or
- The average of a three-year period calculated on the basis of the previous five-year period, excluding the highest and the lowest values

## ARTICLE 4 - Modifications to the Nomenclature of the Combined Tariff

When necessary to take account of modifications to the combined nomenclature, the Commission shall be empowered to adopt delegated acts in accordance with Article 227 to adapt the description of products and references contained in this Regulation to the classes or distinctions of the combined nomenclature.

## ARTICLE 5 - Conversion Coefficients for Rice

### 1. Authority

The Commission may issue implementing acts to determine the conversion coefficients for rice at various stages of processing.

### 2. Procedure

Such implementing acts shall be adopted in accordance with the examination procedure referred to in Article 229(2).

## ARTICLE 6 - Marketing Years

The following marketing years are established for the various agricultural products:

- **Cereals:** August 1 to July 31
- **Rice:** September 1 to August 31
- **Olive oil:** November 1 to October 31
- **Wine:** September 1 to August 31

## ARTICLE 225 - Reporting Obligations

The Commission shall submit reports to the European Parliament and to the Council:

- By December 31, 2025, and subsequently every seven years, on the application of competition rules in agriculture in all Member States
- By December 31, 2023, on EU market observatories established in accordance with Article 222a
- By December 31, 2023, and subsequently every three years, on the use of crisis measures
- By December 31, 2024, on the use of new information and communication technologies to ensure better market transparency
- By June 30, 2024, on denominations of sales and classification of carcasses in the ovine meat sector

## PART VI - POWERS, IMPLEMENTING PROVISIONS, TRANSITIONAL AND FINAL PROVISIONS

### CHAPTER I - Powers and Implementing Provisions

## ARTICLE 227 - Exercise of the Delegation

### 1. Conferral of Power

The power to adopt delegated acts is conferred on the Commission under the conditions set out in this Article.

### 2. Duration of Delegation

The power to adopt delegated acts conferred by this Regulation shall be granted for a period of seven years from December 20, 2013. The Commission shall draw up a report in respect of the delegation of power at the latest nine months before the end of the seven-year period. The delegation of power shall be tacitly extended for periods of an identical duration, unless the European Parliament or the Council expresses its opposition within three months before the end of each period.

### 3. Revocation

The delegation referred to in this Regulation may be revoked at any time by the European Parliament or the Council. A decision to revoke shall put an end to the delegation of power specified in that decision. It shall take effect on the day following that of publication of the decision in the Official Journal of the European Union or on a later date specified therein. It shall not affect the validity of delegated acts already in force.

### 4. Publication and Notification

As soon as it adopts a delegated act, the Commission shall notify it simultaneously to the European Parliament and the Council.

### 5. Entry into Force

A delegated act adopted pursuant to this Regulation shall enter into force only if no objection has been expressed by the European Parliament or the Council within two months from the date of notification of that act or if, before the expiry of that period, both the European Parliament and the Council have informed the Commission that they will not raise objections. The period shall be extended by two months on the initiative of the European Parliament or the Council.

## ARTICLE 228 - Urgent Necessity Procedure

### 1. Immediate Application

A delegated act adopted in accordance with this Article shall enter into force immediately and shall apply if no objection has been raised in accordance with paragraph 2. The notification of the delegated act to the European Parliament and the Council shall state the reasons for the use of the urgent procedure.

### 2. Objection Procedure

The European Parliament or the Council may raise objections to the delegated act adopted pursuant to this Article in accordance with the procedure laid down in Article 227(5). Should the European Parliament or the Council raise objections, the Commission shall repeal the act immediately upon receipt of notification of the objection decision from the European Parliament or the Council.
