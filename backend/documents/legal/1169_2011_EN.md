# EU Regulation 1169/2011 - Food Information to Consumers

## Introduction

Regulation (EU) No. 1169/2011 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 25 October 2011 establishes a comprehensive framework for providing information about food to consumers. The regulation modifies previous directives and introduces modern labeling requirements that reflect consumer needs for transparency and accurate information.

The document presented constitutes a documentation tool and does not have legal force. The authentic texts are published in the Official Journal of the European Union (OJ L 304 of 22.11.2011, p. 18).

## Basic Content

### Purpose and Application

The regulation sets requirements for providing food information to consumers, aiming to:

- Protect the health and interests of consumers
- Provide accurate, clear, and understandable information
- Empower consumers to make informed choices

### Main Amendments to Regulations

This regulation amends:
- Regulation (EC) No. 1924/2006 on health claims
- Regulation (EC) No. 1925/2006 on added vitamins and minerals

And repeals:
- Directive 87/250/EEC of the Commission
- Directive 90/496/EEC of the Council
- Directive 1999/10/EC of the Commission
- Directive 2000/13/EC of the European Parliament and Council
- Directives 2002/67/EC and 2008/5/EC of the Commission
- Regulation (EC) No. 608/2004 of the Commission

## Key Information for Consumers

The regulation establishes obligations for:

1. **Mandatory Information** that must be provided for all pre-packed foods
2. **Food Name** in a clear and definitely visible manner
3. **List of Ingredients** with description of allergens
4. **Quantitative Declaration of Ingredients** (QUID) where required
5. **Net Weight or Volume** of the food
6. **Date of Expiry or Consumption Date**
7. **Storage Conditions** and instructions for use
8. **Business Details** (name and address)

## Purpose of the Regulation

The regulation aims to ensure that:
- Consumers have access to accurate information
- Labeling is clear and understandable
- Misleading practices are prevented
- Public health is protected

## Scope of Application

The regulation applies to:
- All pre-packed foods
- Foods placed on the market in the European Union
- Foods that are imported or exported

The regulation does not apply to:
- Non-pre-packed foods (unless national rules are adopted)
- Foods packaged at the point of sale or prepared for immediate sale
