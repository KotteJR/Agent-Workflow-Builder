# COMMISSION REGULATION (EU) No. 432/2012

**Date:** May 16, 2012

**Subject:** Establishing a list of permitted health claims made on foods, other than those referring to the reduction of disease risk and to children's development and health

## ARTICLE 1 - Permitted Health Claims

### 1. Scope

The list of health claims that may be made for foods, as referred to in Article 13(3) of Regulation (EC) No. 1924/2006, is determined in the Annex to this Regulation.

### 2. Conditions of Use

The health claims referred to in paragraph 1 may be made for foods in accordance with the conditions laid down in the Annex.

## ARTICLE 2 - Entry into Force and Application

### Effective Date

This Regulation shall enter into force on the twentieth day following its publication in the Official Journal of the European Union.

### Application Date

It shall apply from December 14, 2012.

### Binding Nature

This Regulation shall be binding in its entirety and directly applicable in each Member State.

## ANNEX - LIST OF PERMITTED HEALTH CLAIMS

### Nutrient and Substance Claims

#### Nutrients Covered

The following nutrients, substances, foods or food categories are permitted to bear health claims:

- **Alpha-cyclodextrin:** Helps reduce blood glucose rise after meals containing starch
- **Alpha-linolenic acid (ALA):** Contributes to the maintenance of normal blood cholesterol levels
- **Prunes (dried varieties - Prunus domestica L.):** Contributes to normal bowel function
- **Resistant starch:** Replacing digestible starches with resistant starch contributes to a reduction in the post-prandial rise in blood glucose

### Conditions for Use

#### Alpha-Cyclodextrin
- Must contain at least 5 g per 50 g of starch per portion
- Consumer must be informed that beneficial effects are achieved with consumption as part of a meal
- Reference: EFSA Journal 2012; 10(6):2713

#### ALA (Alpha-linolenic acid)
- Must be a source of omega-3 fatty acids as claimed
- Daily intake of 2 g ALA required for beneficial effect
- Reference: EFSA Journal 2009; 7(9):1252, 2011; 9(6):2203

#### Dried Prunes
- Must provide daily intake of 100 g of prunes
- Consumer must be informed that beneficial effects are achieved with daily consumption of 100 g
- Reference: EFSA Journal 2012; 10(6):2712
