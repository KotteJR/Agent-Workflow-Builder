# EU Regulation 1308/2013 - Common Organization of Agricultural Markets

## Introduction

Regulation (EU) No. 1308/2013 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 17 December 2013 establishes the common organization of the markets in agricultural products. It constitutes the fundamental legal framework for the Common Agricultural Policy (CAP) of the European Union and defines the rules for market intervention in agricultural products.

The regulation was published in OJ L 347 of 20.12.2013, p. 671 and is a documentation tool that is updated regularly.

## Scope of Application

### Agricultural Products

The regulation establishes common market organization for all products included in Annex I of the Treaties, with the exception of:
- Fishery and aquaculture products

### Sectors of Application

Agricultural products are subdivided into the following sectors:

| Sector | Part | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Cereals | Part I | Wheat, barley, maize, etc. |
| Rice | Part II | Rice and derived products |
| Sugar | Part III | White and raw sugar |
| Dried Fodder | Part IV | Herbaceous fodder |
| Seeds for Sowing | Part V | Seeds for agricultural production |
| Hops | Part VI | Hops for beer |
| Olive Oil | Part VII | Olive oil and table olives |
| Flax and Hemp | Part VIII | Plant fibers |
| Vegetables and Fruit | Part IX | Fruits and vegetables |
| Bananas | Part XI | Tropical fruits |
| Wine | Part XII | Wine |
| Beef and Veal | Part XV | Slaughtered bovines |
| Milk and Dairy | Part XVI | Dairy products |
| Pigmeat | Part XVII | Slaughtered pigs |
| Sheepmeat and Goatmeat | Part XVIII | Slaughtered goats/sheep |
| Eggs | Part XIX | Chicken eggs |
| Poultry Meat | Part XX | Chicken and other poultry |

## Marketing Periods

Different marketing periods are established for various product categories:

- **1 January - 31 December**: Vegetables and fruit, processed vegetables and fruit, bananas
- **1 April - 31 March**: Dried fodder, silkworms
- **1 July - 30 June**: Cereals, seeds, flax/hemp, milk
- **1 August - 31 July**: Wine sector
- **1 September - 31 August**: Rice, table olives
- **1 October - 30 September**: Sugar, olive oil

## Market Interventions

### Public Intervention

The regulation provides for market intervention in the form of:
- Public intervention (public buying)
- Private storage aid

### Purpose

Interventions aim to:
- Stabilize markets
- Support agricultural community
- Ensure farmer incomes

## General CAP Provisions

For measures provided in this regulation, Regulation (EU) 2021/2116 applies regarding:
- Financing
- Management
- Monitoring of the Common Agricultural Policy

## Definitions

The regulation contains detailed definitions for:
- **Less developed regions**: Regions as defined in related regulation annexes
- **Adverse weather phenomena**: Conditions such as frost, hail, ice that destroy more than 30% of production

## Commission Reporting Requirements

The regulation establishes the Commission's obligation to submit reports on:
- Application of competition rules in agriculture (every seven years)
- Use of market crisis measures (every three years)
- EU market observatories
- New technologies for market transparency

## Important Notes

1. The regulation replaces previous legal texts on agricultural markets
2. Provides a unified framework for all EU member states
3. Ensures fair competitive order
4. Supports the agricultural community
5. Is regularly updated to adapt to changing market conditions
