# IMPLEMENTING REGULATION (EU) 2018/775

**Date:** May 28, 2018

**Subject:** Rules for the implementation of Article 26(3) of Regulation (EU) No. 1169/2011 on the country of origin or place of provenance of the primary ingredient of a food

## ARTICLE 1 - SCOPE

### 1. Application

This Regulation specifies the detailed rules for implementing Article 26(3) of Regulation (EU) No. 1169/2011 when the country of origin or place of provenance of a food is indicated in any form, including statements, graphic representations, symbols or terms referring to places or geographical areas.

### 2. Exceptions

This Regulation does not apply to:

- **Protected Geographical Indications:** Those protected under Regulation (EU) No. 1151/2012, Regulation (EU) No. 1308/2013, Regulation (EC) No. 110/2008, or Regulation (EU) No. 251/2014
- **International Agreements:** Geographical indications protected under international agreements
- **Trade Marks:** Registered trade marks when they constitute an indication of origin (pending specific rules)

## ARTICLE 2 - INDICATION OF COUNTRY OF ORIGIN OR PLACE OF PROVENANCE

### When Origin Differs from Primary Ingredient

The country of origin or place of provenance of the primary ingredient that differs from the country of origin or place of provenance indicated on the label shall be indicated as follows:

#### Option (a) - Geographical Regions

By reference to one of the following geographical areas:

**i) EU/Non-EU Regions:**
- "EU" (European Union)
- "Non-EU"
- "EU and Non-EU"

**ii) Specific Regions:**
- A region or other geographical area within Member States or third countries, if defined in public international law or easily understood by the average consumer with normal attention

**iii) Fishing Zones:**
- FAO fishing zones or other marine or freshwater organization zones, if defined in international law or easily understood by the average consumer with normal attention

**iv) Country References:**
- Member State(s) or third country(ies)
- A region or other geographical area within a Member State or third country, easily understood by the average consumer with normal attention

**v) Special Union Provisions:**
- Country of origin or place of provenance according to specific Union legislation applicable to that particular primary ingredient

#### Option (b) - Statement Format

By using a statement such as:

> "[Name of primary ingredient] does not originate from [country of origin or place of provenance of the food]"

Or any similar statement likely to convey the same meaning to the consumer.

## ARTICLE 3 - PRESENTATION OF INFORMATION

### 1. Font Size Requirements

Information provided according to Article 2 must be displayed in a font size that is not smaller than the minimum size required under Article 13(2) of Regulation (EU) No. 1169/2011.

### 2. Presentation with Text Indication

When the country of origin or place of provenance of a food is indicated in text form, the information provided must:

- Appear in the same visual field as the country of origin indication
- Use a font height at least 75% of the height of the country of origin indication

### 3. Presentation with Non-Text Indication

When the country of origin or place of provenance is indicated in non-text form (graphic, symbol), the information provided must:

- Appear in the same visual field as the country of origin indication

## ARTICLE 4 - ENTRY INTO FORCE AND APPLICATION

### Effective Date

This Regulation entered into force on the third day following publication in the Official Journal of the European Union.

### Application Date

Application commenced on **April 1, 2020.**

### Transitional Provisions

Foods placed on the market or labeled before the application date may continue to circulate until stocks are exhausted.

### Binding Effect

This Regulation is binding in its entirety and directly applicable in each Member State.

## PURPOSE AND CONTEXT

### Background

Article 26 of Regulation (EU) No. 1169/2011 (Food Information to Consumers) requires that when the country of origin or place of provenance of a food is indicated on the label, the country of origin or place of provenance of the primary ingredient must also be indicated if it differs from that of the food.

### Consumer Information

This Regulation ensures consumers have clear and accessible information about:

- Where the primary ingredient of a food originates
- Whether primary ingredients come from within or outside the EU
- The geographical area of origin when different from the finished product

### Market Transparency

The rules promote:

- Fair competition among food businesses
- Consumer awareness about food composition
- Transparency in food labeling
- Prevention of misleading information

## PRACTICAL APPLICATION EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Italian Pasta with Non-EU Durum Wheat

**Label Indication:**
> "Durum Wheat Origin: Non-EU"

Or:

> "Durum wheat does not originate from the EU"

### Example 2: Spanish Olive Oil with Non-EU Olives

**Label Indication:**
> "Olives Origin: Non-EU and EU"

Or:

> "Olives originate from non-EU and EU countries"

### Example 3: French Wine with Imported Grapes

**Label Indication:**
> "Grapes: Origin - Argentina"

Or:

> "Grapes do not originate from France"

## COMPLIANCE AND ENFORCEMENT

### Requirements for Food Business Operators

Food business operators must:

- Ensure accurate information about primary ingredient origin
- Maintain documentation supporting origin claims
- Update labeling to reflect actual sources
- Comply with application deadlines
- Ensure font and presentation requirements are met

### Member State Oversight

Member States shall:

- Monitor compliance with this Regulation
- Enforce requirements through appropriate sanctions
- Take action against non-compliant products
- Support food business operators with guidance

## SCOPE OF PRIMARY INGREDIENTS

### Definition

A "primary ingredient" means the ingredient or ingredients of a food that constitute more than 50% of that food by weight or the ingredient listed first in the ingredient list if no ingredient exceeds 50%.

### Determination

The primary ingredient is identified based on:

- Actual composition of the food
- Ingredient list requirements under Regulation (EU) No. 1169/2011
