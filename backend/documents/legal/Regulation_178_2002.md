# REGULATION (EC) No. 178/2002

**Date:** January 28, 2002

**Subject:** Laying down the general principles and requirements of food law, establishing the European Food Safety Authority and laying down procedures in matters of food safety

## PART I - GENERAL PRINCIPLES

## ARTICLE 1 - OBJECTIVE

This Regulation lays down general principles and requirements of food law, establishes the European Food Safety Authority (EFSA), and lays down procedures in matters of food safety with the aim of protecting human health and consumers' interests, taking into account in particular:

- Food safety and integrity
- Animal health and welfare
- Plant health
- The environment
- Fair practices in food trade

## CHAPTER I - SCOPE AND DEFINITIONS

## ARTICLE 2 - SCOPE

### 1. General Application

This Regulation applies to:

- All stages of production, processing and distribution of food
- All stages of production, processing and distribution of feed
- Imported food and feed

### 2. Exclusions

This Regulation does not apply to:

- Primary production for private domestic use
- Food prepared for private domestic consumption

### 3. Special Cases

Specific provisions may apply to particular sectors as determined by the European Commission.

## ARTICLE 3 - KEY DEFINITIONS

### Definitions

For the purposes of this Regulation:

**Food:** Any substance or product, whether processed, partially processed or unprocessed, intended to be, or reasonably expected to be, ingested by humans. This includes drink, chewing gum and any substance, including water, intentionally incorporated into food during its manufacture, preparation or treatment.

**Feed:** Any substance or product, including additives, intended for animal feeding by oral ingestion.

**Food Law:** The laws, regulations and administrative provisions governing food in general, and food safety in particular, at European Union or Member State level. It applies to all stages of production, processing and distribution of food.

**Food Business:** Any undertaking, whether for profit or not and whether public or private, carrying out any of the activities related to any stage of production, processing and distribution of food.

**Food Business Operator:** The natural or legal person responsible to the competent authority for ensuring that food law requirements are met.

**Primary Production:** The production, rearing or cultivation of primary products including harvesting, milking, and transportation of live animals.

**Hazard:** A biological, chemical or physical agent in food, or condition of food, with the potential to cause an adverse health effect.

**Risk:** A function of the probability of an adverse health effect and the severity of that effect, consequential to a hazard.

## ARTICLE 4 - GENERAL PRINCIPLES

### Transparency and Information

Food business operators and competent authorities shall provide consumers with the information they need to:

- Make informed choices
- Understand food production and safety measures
- Know about any risks associated with food consumption

### Traceability

**One Step Back/One Step Forward:**
Food business operators shall be able to identify:
- The supplier of food or feed
- The immediate customer of their products

**Full Traceability:**
Competent authorities shall require traceability throughout the food chain when necessary to protect public health.

### Control and Responsibility

**Prevention First:**
Food business operators shall:
- Identify potential hazards
- Implement preventive measures
- Maintain control through documented procedures (HACCP principles)

**Responsibility and Accountability:**
Food business operators are responsible for ensuring compliance with food law and shall maintain documentation to demonstrate this.

## PART II - FOOD SAFETY REQUIREMENTS

## ARTICLE 5 - GENERAL FOOD SAFETY REQUIREMENTS

### Basic Obligation

Food shall not be placed on the market if it is unsafe.

### Food Safety Definition

Food is safe when it is fit for human consumption and does not cause illness or injury to consumers.

## ARTICLE 6 - CONSUMER PROTECTION FROM HAZARDS

### Prohibited Practices

Food shall not be placed on the market if it:

- Contains biological agents or their toxins at levels that are potentially harmful to health
- Contains pesticide residues or contaminants in excess of permitted limits
- Is below safe microbiological limits
- Is below safe chemical or physical safety standards

### Temperature Control

Where applicable, food shall be:

- Produced and handled at appropriate temperatures
- Stored and transported under proper conditions
- Protected against contamination

## ARTICLE 7 - FOODSTUFF INFORMATION AND TRACEABILITY

### Labeling Requirements

Food business operators shall provide information about:

- Identity and properties of the food
- Nutritional composition (where required)
- Potential risks to specific consumer groups
- Instructions for safe use and storage

### Record Keeping

Food business operators shall maintain records of:

- Sources of raw materials and ingredients
- Suppliers of feed ingredients
- Customers of food and feed products
- Processing records
- Temperature and humidity monitoring
- Cleaning and pest control
- Employee training records

## ARTICLE 8 - HAZARD ANALYSIS AND CRITICAL CONTROL POINTS (HACCP)

### Requirements

Food business operators shall:

- Identify potential hazards
- Determine critical control points (CCPs)
- Establish critical limits for each CCP
- Monitor CCPs
- Establish corrective actions
- Verify and validate HACCP procedures
- Keep records and documentation

### HACCP Principles

1. **Identify hazards:** Biological, chemical, and physical
2. **Determine critical control points:** Steps where control can be applied
3. **Establish critical limits:** Boundaries between safe and unsafe conditions
4. **Establish monitoring procedures:** For each CCP
5. **Establish corrective actions:** When limits are exceeded
6. **Establish verification procedures:** To ensure HACCP effectiveness
7. **Establish documentation:** To demonstrate compliance

## PART III - COMPETENT AUTHORITIES

## ARTICLE 9 - DESIGNATION OF COMPETENT AUTHORITIES

### Member State Obligation

Each Member State shall designate competent authorities responsible for:

- Implementation of food law
- Official controls
- Investigation of food-related incidents
- Enforcement of requirements

### Authority Functions

Competent authorities shall:

- Conduct official controls
- Monitor food business operator compliance
- Take enforcement action
- Report to higher authorities
- Cooperate with other Member States and the Commission

## PART IV - EUROPEAN FOOD SAFETY AUTHORITY

## ARTICLE 10 - ESTABLISHMENT OF THE EFSA

### Objectives

The European Food Safety Authority is established to:

- Provide scientific advice on food safety
- Perform risk assessments
- Provide rapid response to food safety crises
- Support Member States
- Communicate about food safety risks

### Tasks

The EFSA shall:

- Assess scientific evidence
- Provide opinions on food safety matters
- Advise the Commission and Member States
- Coordinate with international organizations
- Maintain scientific databases

## PART V - CRISIS MANAGEMENT

## ARTICLE 11 - EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

### Food Safety Alerts

When a serious risk to human health is detected:

- The affected Member State notifies the Commission
- The Commission informs other Member States
- Rapid Risk Assessment is conducted
- Coordinated action is taken

### Measures

Authorities may:

- Issue recalls
- Implement trade restrictions
- Establish temporary import bans
- Mandate reformulation or relabeling
- Suspend marketing

## PART VI - INFORMATION AND CONSUMER COMMUNICATION

## ARTICLE 12 - INFORMATION TO CONSUMERS

### Right to Information

Consumers have the right to:

- Know about food composition and properties
- Understand food production methods
- Be informed of food safety measures
- Learn about potential risks

### Communication

Food business operators and authorities shall:

- Provide clear and understandable information
- Respond to consumer inquiries
- Issue warnings about hazards
- Use accessible communication methods

## ARTICLE 13 - TRANSPARENCY

### General Principle

Food law shall be applied with a high degree of transparency to:

- Protect public health
- Maintain consumer confidence
- Ensure fair competition
- Facilitate international trade

### Information Exchange

The Commission shall maintain databases and systems for:

- Sharing information between Member States
- Reporting food safety incidents
- Coordinating rapid response
- Monitoring trends

## ENTRY INTO FORCE

This Regulation entered into force on February 28, 2002, and has been the foundational food law regulation for the European Union since then.
