# EU Regulation 2018/848 - Organic Production and Labeling

## Introduction

Regulation (EU) 2018/848 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 30 May 2018 establishes the principles of **organic production** and the rules regarding organic production, related certification, and the use of indications related to organic production in labeling and advertising.

The regulation was published in OJ L 150 of 14.6.2018, p. 1 and repeals the previous Regulation (EC) No. 834/2007.

## Scope of Application

### Products Covered

This regulation applies to products originating from the agricultural sector, including:

1. **Primary Production Products**:
   - Live or unprocessed agricultural products
   - Seeds and other plant reproductive material
   - Aquaculture products
   - Beekeeping products

2. **Processed Products**:
   - Processed agricultural products for use as food
   - Products included in Annex I

3. **Animal Feed**:
   - Animal feed for use in organic production

4. **Related Products**:
   - Products closely linked to agriculture
   - As referred to in Annex I of the regulation
   - E.g. essential oils, yeast

### Exclusions from Scope

**Mass catering** (catering):
- Not fully subject to the regulation
- Member states may apply national rules
- The EU organic production logo is not used

## Subject Matter of the Regulation

The regulation establishes:

1. **Principles of Organic Production**:
   - Conservation of biodiversity
   - Preservation of soil quality
   - Prevention and control of harmful organisms
   - Avoidance of negative environmental impacts

2. **Production Rules**:
   - At all stages of production
   - Processing and distribution

3. **Certification**:
   - Organic product certification procedures
   - Controls supplementary to Regulation (EU) 2017/625 rules

4. **Labeling and Advertising**:
   - Use of organic production indications
   - EU logo for organic production
   - Labeling policies

## Key Definitions

### Organic Production
The use of production methods in accordance with the regulation at all stages:
- Production
- Processing
- Distribution
- (Including the conversion period)

### Organic Product
A product originating from organic production, **excluding** products produced during the conversion period.

**Note**: Wild-caught fish or game are not considered organic products.

### Agricultural Raw Material
An agricultural product that has not been subject to any preservation or processing work.

### Conversion
The transition from non-organic to organic production within a certain time period during which the regulation's provisions are applied.

### Farm/Holding
All production units operating under single management with the aim of producing:
- Live or unprocessed agricultural products
- Aquaculture products
- Beekeeping products

## Production Units

### Organic Production Unit
A production unit (outside conversion period) managed in accordance with organic production requirements.

### Conversion Unit
A production unit during the conversion period managed in accordance with organic production requirements. May consist of fields or other assets with different conversion start dates.

### Non-Organic Production Unit
A production unit not managed in accordance with organic production requirements.

## Plant and Animal Products

### Plant Production
Production of agricultural crop products, including harvesting of wild plants for commercial purposes.

### Animal Production
Production of:
- Domesticated or tamed terrestrial animals
- Including insects

### Veranda/Covered Area
An additional, unheated, non-insulated external covered area of a building for poultry, typically wire-netted, open to external weather conditions, with natural and artificial lighting and bedded floor.

### Laying Hens
Animals of the species *Gallus gallus* intended for egg production at least 18 weeks of age.

## Plant Reproductive Material

### Organic Heterogeneous Material
A group of plants within a single botanical taxonomic unit that:
- Exhibits common phenotypic characteristics
- Is characterized by high genetic and phenotypic diversity level
- Is not a variety as defined in Regulation (EC) 2100/94
- Does not constitute a mixture of varieties
- Has been produced in accordance with this regulation

### Organic Variety Suitable for Organic Production
A variety that:
- Is characterized by high genetic and phenotypic diversity level
- Results from organic breeding activities

## Ingredients and Raw Materials

### Ingredient
- For food: as defined in Regulation (EU) 1169/2011
- For other products: any substance or product used in preparation/processing that continues to exist in the final product (even in modified form)

### Animal Feed Raw Materials
As defined in Regulation (EC) No. 767/2009

## Traceability

The ability to identify and track:
- Food products
- Animal feed
- Organic production products
- At all stages of production, processing and distribution

## Enterprise

A natural or legal person responsible for ensuring compliance with the regulation at all stages of production, processing and distribution that it controls.

## Important Information

1. The regulation has broad scope of application covering all production stages
2. Provides unified legal framework for organic products in the EU
3. Includes detailed definitions of relevant terms
4. Establishes obligations for businesses and member states
5. Is regularly updated with delegated regulations
